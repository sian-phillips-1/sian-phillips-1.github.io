Childcare Costs Project

This project was made during my master's degree in response to a brief set out by the West Midlands Combined Authority. The aim: to convey was that childcare costs are disproportionately borne by the most deprived households, and the infographics and visualisations should make visible the challenges of this.

The Process

To execute the aim of the project I wanted to utilise the method of comparison to channel the focus of the audience. The story in the final outcome revolves around two mothers in the West Midlands one living in a deprived area with high childcare costs and one living in an area with low deprivation and low childcare costs. I gave the characters names and pictures to humanise the data points and emphasise the ‘realness’ of the issue.

The audience for my final visualisation would primarily be the public, but the visual could also be used by organisations that are looking to help parents that are struggling with their childcare costs.

R was used for cleaning, analysing and plotting all data. Canva was used for the finishing touches. The people illustrations were from the collection available in Canva.
