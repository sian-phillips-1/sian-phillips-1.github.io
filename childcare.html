Childcare Costs Project

This infographic was made to convey the challenges of childcare costs being disproportionately borne by the most deprived households. The brief was set out by the West Midlands Combined Authority.

The Process

To execute the aim of the project I wanted to utilise the method of comparison to channel the focus of the audience. The story in the final outcome revolves around two mothers in the West Midlands, one living in a deprived area with high childcare costs and one living in an area with low deprivation and low childcare costs. I gave the characters names and pictures to humanise the data points and emphasise the ‘realness’ of the issue.

R was used for cleaning, analysing and plotting all data. Canva was used for the finishing touches. The illustrations of the mothers were from the collection of icons available in Canva.

The basic charts, that were produced in R, can be seen on the right. The packages used included:

  • dplyr: for cleaning the data.
  • ggplot: for plotting the donut chart, the line chart and the scatter plot.
  • RcolorBrewer: for creating a unique colour palatte to use in the plots.
  • sf: for encoding spacial vector data of the IMD scores and childcare costs for each area in the West Midlands.
The colour scheme and fonts used were replicated from the offical West Midlands design template.
