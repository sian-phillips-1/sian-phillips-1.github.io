The Personal is Political

The aim: To pick a theme/issue and explore it through both the data I collected in my personal data journal and a dataset from an external source.

The Process

I chose to explore the theme of cycling, specifically the attitudes towards it, gathering data from my own personal experience and comparing to wider society. I wanted to investigate the reasons why people decide not to cycle and what may encourage more people to do so. This is an issue I feel is both important to me personally as it is something I enjoy but also important to society and the environment, as cycling is a way to reduce carbon emissions and live a healthier life. Through my experiences I could compare factors important to me in my trips with other people. The visualisation aims to give insight to inspire change.

To collect my personal data, I used photographs to keep track of my cycle trips, taking a photo of where I parked my bike and a photo when I reached my destination. On my phone I edited the photograph and added my data as text. This gave me options when it came to visualising the data, as I had access to the GPS locations, time stamps, and visual depictions to work with.

The final layout of the visualisation consists of four circular diagrams that resemble wheels of a bike, with data encoded through lines symbolising spokes. This design choice was inspired by Federica Frangapane’s work, which employs shape and colour to present data in intricate patterns. Figure 7 shows one of her visualisations which I used to help guide me, as it has characteristics of a bike wheel.

To further continue the theme of cycling and transport I used a line symbolic of a road to split the page between the personal and external data. Overall, the visualisations involve a level of complexity that might take time to understand the different components. This was the intention, drawing from Giorgia Lupi’s standpoint of embracing complexity, taken from her Data Humanism Manifesto (PrintMag, 2017). Following this suggestion, I worked against the idea that the visualisation should be understood at a glance, and aimed my piece at an audience that would be open to spending longer with the information.

R was used for cleaning, analysing and plotting all data. Canva for the finishing touches. The people icons were from the Canva collection.
